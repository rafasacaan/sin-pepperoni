Find satellite data

We need satellite data to support any analysis on data. Here is a list for the top satellite maps in 2024: - Satellite maps sources

Sentinel images is a good option! There are six Sentinel missions, where three can be accessed through its API. Let´s check the description for each set of images:

Sentinel-1

  • Applications: Sentinel-1 is used for monitoring changes in land cover, detecting deforestation, mapping floods, monitoring sea ice, and supporting maritime surveillance.
  • Information: Sentinel-1 is a radar imaging satellite that provides all-weather, day-and-night radar imagery.
  • Resolution: Sentinel-1 offers various resolution modes, including: Interferometric Wide swath mode (IW): 5 m x 20 m (VV and VH polarizations) Extra Wide swath mode (EW): 20 m x 40 m (HH polarization)
  • Stripmap mode (SM): 5 m x 5 m (VV and VH polarizations)

Sentinel-2

  • Information: Sentinel-2 is a multispectral optical imaging satellite, providing high-resolution imagery in 13 spectral bands.
  • Resolution: Sentinel-2 offers the following spatial resolutions:
    • 10 meters for the visible and near-infrared bands
    • 20 meters for the red edge and shortwave infrared bands
    • 60 meters for the atmospheric and water vapor bands
  • Applications: Sentinel-2 is widely used for monitoring agriculture, forestry, land cover changes, urban planning, and environmental monitoring.

Sentinel-3:

  • Information: Sentinel-3 is a multi-instrument satellite mission designed for oceanographic and land monitoring applications.
  • Resolution: Sentinel-3 provides different resolution characteristics depending on the instrument:
    • Sea and Land Surface Temperature Radiometer (SLSTR): Spatial resolution varies from 500 m to 1 km
    • Ocean and Land Colour Instrument (OLCI): Spatial resolution ranges from 300 m to 1 km
    • Synthetic Aperture Radar Altimeter (SRAL): Spatial resolution is around 300 m over the ocean
  • Applications: Sentinel-3 is primarily used for monitoring ocean ecosystems, sea surface temperature, ocean color, sea level changes, land surface temperature, and vegetation health.
